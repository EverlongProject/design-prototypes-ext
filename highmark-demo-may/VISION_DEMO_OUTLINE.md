# Vision Demo: Use Cases Walked Through With Highmark

**Persona:** Jessica, 47, Highmark West Virginia member. Two sons on her plan (Liam 18, varsity soccer; Noah 14). Mid-workday at her desk.

**One-line arc:** AI proactively texts Jessica about her overdue colonoscopy. She opens MyHighmark and pivots to her son's hamstring injury. The AI books Liam's PT at AHN Wexford, then circles back to close the loop on Jessica's colonoscopy by booking her at the same facility for a date that respects the family's care calendar. Two HEDIS gaps closed in one session.

**Total runtime:** ~7 minutes. **All four agent teams featured.**

## Use cases, in sequence

**1. Proactive multi-channel outreach.** Care Gap Agent texts Jessica about her overdue colonoscopy with a deep link to MyHighmark.
*Demonstrates:* Care Gap Agent SMS outreach, deep-link to authenticated experience.

**2. AI as the front door, not a sidebar.** Member lands on MyHighmark and the AI sidebar opens to continue the conversation seeded by the SMS.
*Demonstrates:* Continuous context across channels, Health Story persistence.

**3. Multimodal explainer with embedded video.** Agent introduces the colonoscopy topic and shows a short Highmark-branded explainer video inline in the conversation.
*Demonstrates:* Health Coach Agent's educational role, multimodal AI response, member health literacy support.

**4. Free-text pivot from outreach topic to actual member need.** Jessica types: *"My son's doctor told me to book him with a physical therapist after he hurt himself playing soccer."*
*Demonstrates:* AI handles unstructured intent, adapts mid-conversation, doesn't lose track of the original mission.

**5. Family / dependent disambiguation.** Agent asks which son, presents two tappable cards (Liam 18 and Noah 14). Member taps Liam.
*Demonstrates:* AI knows the member's family, manages dependent context cleanly, doesn't guess.

**6. Coverage check grounded in real per-member data.** Agent surfaces a coverage snapshot card for Liam: deductible already met, $25 PT copay, sports injury covered, no extra referral needed since Dr. Martinez's referral came in Tuesday.
*Demonstrates:* Benefits Navigation Agent + per-dependent accumulator integration + clinical data integration (referrals).

**7. Caregiver consent in one line.** Agent confirms *"I have access to Liam's records as the primary caregiver on his plan."*
*Demonstrates:* HIPAA-respectful caregiver workflow handled cleanly without a friction-heavy consent flow.

**8. Partner solution awareness (the lost-in-network solve).** Agent offers two paths: in-person PT or Sword Health virtual PT, covered at $0. Cost framing visible ($0 vs ~$300 in copays). Member picks in-person.
*Demonstrates:* Health Coach Agent surfacing Living Health solutions at the relevant moment, the central HSA deck thesis demonstrated in real time.

**9. In-network provider search with visible network steerage.** Map view plus three sports PT provider cards, sorted Tier 1 first. Tier 1 vs Tier 2 cost difference visible without being preachy.
*Demonstrates:* Care Navigation Agent (Phase 1), Kyruus integration, network steerage as a visual default.

**10. Iterative search refinement.** Member filters to weekend availability. Map re-pins, cards animate to filtered results.
*Demonstrates:* AI handles multi-turn refinement without losing context.

**11. One-tap booking with calendar integration.** Liam's PT booked: Dr. Patel, AHN Sports & Spine Wexford, Saturday May 9 at 10am. Calendar invite sent to jessica@company.com.
*Demonstrates:* Real scheduling integration, end-to-end booking.

**12. Closing the loop on the original outreach.** Agent returns to Jessica's colonoscopy with a clear "Closing the loop" beat: confirms the procedure is fully covered preventive care, asks whether she wants the same facility (AHN Wexford) she'll already know from Liam's PT, or a different one. Jessica picks "same facility."
*Demonstrates:* AI doesn't lose the original mission, names the topic shift explicitly, gives the member real autonomy over the choice instead of forcing it.

**13. Member-led slot selection.** Agent surfaces three weekday morning slots with Dr. Sarah Chen at AHN Wexford. Jessica picks Tuesday May 12 at 8am.
*Demonstrates:* AI presents real options with realistic timing (weekday slots that respect the prep + sedation reality of a colonoscopy), lets the member choose, doesn't dictate.

**14. Two HEDIS gaps closed in one session.** Colonoscopy booked at AHN Wexford for Tuesday May 12, 8am, $0 (preventive). Calendar invite sent. Prep plan will land in her inbox the week before.
*Demonstrates:* Care Gap Agent closes its original loop, Care Navigation executes the booking, all four agent teams have now contributed to a single member outcome.

**15. Tally reveal.** Final frame summarizing: PT booked Saturday at AHN Wexford for Liam, colonoscopy booked Tuesday at the same AHN Wexford for Jessica, two HEDIS gaps closed, zero phone calls, zero portal logins, zero forms, ~7 minutes total.

## Agent teams featured

- **Care Gap Agent:** opens the loop (SMS outreach), closes the loop (colonoscopy booking at the end with a clear "closing the loop" beat)
- **Health Coach Agent:** surfaces Sword Health, presents virtual vs in-person paths
- **Benefits Navigation Agent:** coverage check, deductible status, plan documents, sports injury PT eligibility
- **Care Navigation Agent:** sports PT provider search, scheduling, gastroenterology booking

## Business outcomes implied

- HEDIS gap closure (colonoscopy booked, Liam's covered PT engaged)
- Network steerage to Tier 1 AHN providers, made visible
- Living Health solution awareness (Sword Health surfaced and seen, even if not selected this time)
- Call center deflection (entire interaction self-served)
- Network continuity across the family (same facility used for both members' care)
- Caregiver / family workflow support (most demos treat the member as the only patient; this one doesn't)

## What this demo proves to Tracy and Stacy

- The AI is **adaptive, not scripted.** The typed pivot from colonoscopy to PT is the proof point.
- The AI **knows the family, not just the primary member.** Dependent disambiguation makes it visible.
- The AI **doesn't lose track of the original mission**, even after a member detour. The "Closing the loop" beat names the return explicitly.
- The AI **orchestrates across all four agent teams** in one conversation, in one session.
- The AI **respects member autonomy on timing**, doesn't force care onto someone else's calendar (no medically unrealistic same-day procedure stacking).
